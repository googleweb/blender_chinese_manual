Markers¶

Markers are used to denote frames at which something significant happens - it could be that a character’s animation starts, the camera changes position, or a door opens, for example. Markers can be given names to make them more meaningful at a quick glance. They are available in many of Blender’s editors.

Note

Unlike the keyframes, markers are always placed at a whole frame number, you cannot set a marker at frame 2.5.

Markers can be created and edited in all of the following editors:

  • The Graph Editor.
  • The Action Editor.
  • The The Dope Sheet.
  • The NLA Editor.
  • The 视频序列编辑器.
  • The The Timeline.

Note

A marker created in one of these editors will also appear in all others that support them.

Visualization¶

Standard¶

Markers: small but useful.

Most of the editors visualize markers the same way: as small triangles at their bottom, white if unselected or yellow if selected.

If they have a name, this is shown to their right, in white when the marker is selected.

Sequencer¶

Markers in the Sequencer.

The 视频序列编辑器 just adds a vertical dashed line to each marker (gray if the marker is unselected, or white if it is selected).

3D视图¶

Marker in a 3D视图.

The View does not allow you to create/edit/remove markers, they just show their name between <> at their bottom left corner, near the active object’s name, when you are at their frame (see Marker in a 3D视图).

Creating and 编辑 Markers¶

Creating Markers¶

参考

模式: all modes
菜单: Marker ‣ Add Marker
快捷键: M or Ctrl-Alt-M in the VSE Editor

The simplest way to add a marker is to move to the frame where you would like it to appear, and press M.

Alternatively, you can press Alt-A (or the “playback” button of the Timeline) to make the animation play, and then press M at the appropriate points. This can be especially useful to mark the beats in some music.

选择 Markers¶

参考

模式: all modes
快捷键: RMB

Click RMB on the marker’s triangle to select it. Use Shift-RMB to select multiple markers.

In the Graph Editor, Dope Sheet, NLA Editor, and 视频序列编辑器, you can also select all markers with Ctrl-A, and border-select them with Ctrl-B (as usual, LMB to select, RMB to deselect). The corresponding options are found in the Select menu of these editors.

In the Timeline, you can select all markers with A, and border select with B.

Naming Markers¶

参考

模式: all modes
菜单: Marker ‣ Rename Marker
快捷键: Ctrl-M

Having dozens of markers scattered throughout your scene’s time will not help you much unless you know what they stand for. You can name a marker by selecting it, pressing Ctrl-M, typing the name, and pressing the OK button.

Moving Markers¶

参考

模式: all modes
菜单: Marker ‣ Grab/Move Marker
快捷键: Ctrl-G

Once you have one or more markers selected, press G to move them, and confirm the move with LMB or Return (as usual, cancel the move with RMB, or Esc).

By default, you grab the markers in one-frame steps, but if you hold Ctrl, the markers will move in steps corresponding to one second (according to the scenes FPS).

Duplicating Markers¶

参考

模式: all modes
菜单: Marker ‣ Duplicate Marker
快捷键: Shift-D

You can duplicate the selected markers by pressing Shift-D. Once duplicated, the new ones are automatically placed in grab mode, so you can move them to the desired location.

Note

Note that unlike most other duplications in Blender, the names of the duplicated markers are not altered at all (no .001 numeric counter append).

Deleting Markers¶

参考

模式: all modes
菜单: Marker ‣ Delete Marker
快捷键: X

To delete the selected markers simply press X, and confirm the pop-up message with LMB.

See also

There is another type of markers, called “pose markers”, which are specific to armatures. They are related to the pose libraries, and are discussed in detail here.
