相机 View¶

参考

Mode: All modes
Menu: View ‣ 相机 ‣ Active 相机
Hotkey: Numpad0

相机s View can be used to virtually compose shots and preview how the scene will look when rendered. Pressing Numpad0 will show the scene as viewed from the currently active camera. In this view you can also set the 渲染 Border which defines the portion of the camera view to be rendered.

相机 view provides a preview for the final rendered image.

There are several different ways to navigate and position the camera in your scene, some of them are explained below.

相机 Navigation¶

There are several different ways to navigate and position the camera in your scene, some of them are explained below.

Note

Remember that the active “camera” might be any kind of object. So these actions can be used, for example, to position and aim a lamp.

Move Active 相机 to View¶

参考

Mode: 物体模式
Hotkey: Ctrl-Alt-Numpad0

This feature allows you to position and orient the active camera to match your current viewport.

Select a camera and then move around in the 3D视图 to a desired position and direction for your camera (so that you are seeing what you want the camera to see). Now press Ctrl-Alt-Numpad0 and your selected camera positions itself to match the view, and switches to camera view.

相机 View Positioning¶

By enabling Lock 相机 to View in the View menu of the View Properties panel, while in camera view, you can navigate the 3D视图 as usual, while remaining in camera view. Controls are exactly the same as when normally moving in 3D.

Roll, Pan, Dolly, and Track¶

To perform these camera moves, the camera must first be selected, so that it becomes the active object (while viewing through it, you can RMB -click on the solid rectangular edges to select it). The following actions also assume that you are in camera view Numpad0! Having done so, you can now manipulate the camera using the same commands that are used to manipulate any object:

Roll
Press R to enter object rotation mode. The default will be to rotate the camera in its local Z-axis (the axis orthogonal to the camera view), which is the definition of a camera “roll”.
Vertical Pan or Pitch
This is just a rotation along the local X-axis. Press R to enter object rotation mode, then X twice (the first press selects the global axis - pressing the same letter a second time selects the local axis - this works with any axis; see the axis locking page).
Horizontal Pan or Yaw
This corresponds to a rotation around the camera’s local Y axis... Yes, that is it, press R, and then Y twice!
Dolly
To dolly the camera, press G then MMB (or Z twice).
Sideways Tracking
Press G and move the mouse (you can use X twice or Y to get pure-horizontal or pure-vertical sideways tracking).
